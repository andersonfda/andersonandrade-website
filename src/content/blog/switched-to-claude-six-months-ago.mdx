Six months ago, I moved my entire workflow to Claude. Not as a test. Not as a side experiment. I shut down ChatGPT and went all in.

People thought I was being contrarian. "ChatGPT has the ecosystem," they said. "It has the plugins, the integrations, the brand recognition."

They weren't wrong. But I'd spent 20 years as a network architect. I know what it looks like when the dominant protocol starts losing ground to something technically superior. I've watched it happen with routing protocols, with network architectures, with entire vendor ecosystems. The switch never happens because of one feature. It happens when trust erodes.

Last week, Claude hit #1 on the App Store. Downloads up 60% since January. Paid subscribers doubled since October. And it's not because Anthropic ran a better ad campaign.

It's because OpenAI made a choice. And users made theirs.

## What Actually Happened

OpenAI signed a deal with the Pentagon. Within days, Anthropic got blacklisted by the DoD for refusing to loosen safety guardrails on Claude for military applications.

Read that again. Anthropic got punished for saying no.

The internet noticed. TechCrunch, Axios, Tom's Guide — they all covered the exodus. But the real signal wasn't in the headlines. It was in the App Store rankings. People didn't just tweet about it. They actually switched.

I've been in tech long enough to know that users almost never switch tools voluntarily. The friction is too high. Your history is there. Your muscle memory is there. Your prompts are tuned to the old system. For people to mass-migrate, the push has to be visceral. This was.

## Why I Switched Before the Crowd

My reasons weren't political. They were practical.

I'm building a content automation system — the thing that generated the essay you're reading right now. One command, and it produces platform-specific posts for LinkedIn, X, Threads, Bluesky, Dev.to, Telegram, Discord, and my website. All in my voice. All following my brand rules.

I built the entire thing on Claude Code.

Here's what Claude gives me that ChatGPT didn't:

**Context that holds.** Claude's 200K token context window means I can feed it my entire brand guide — 700 lines of voice rules, audience profiles, platform strategies — and it actually remembers all of it throughout the conversation. With ChatGPT, I was constantly re-explaining who I was.

**Code that ships.** Claude Code doesn't just suggest code. It reads my codebase, understands my patterns, writes files, runs tests, debugs errors. I've built 21 slash commands, 12 API integrations, and a self-critique engine — mostly by describing what I wanted and letting Claude build it. It's not a copilot. It's a cofounder that doesn't need equity.

**Honesty about limits.** When Claude doesn't know something, it says so. When I was debugging an X/Twitter OAuth flow last week, Claude told me the issue was probably on X's side, not in my code. It was right. ChatGPT would have sent me down three rabbit holes of code changes that weren't the problem.

None of this is theoretical. I have 21 drafts, 7 live platform connections, and a discovery engine that scans AI news every morning — all built on Claude in the last six months.

## What This Means If You're Choosing an AI Right Now

If you're a non-technical entrepreneur trying to figure out which AI to invest your time in, here's my honest take:

**ChatGPT is still good for casual use.** Quick questions, image generation, browsing the web. It's the Swiss Army knife. Wide but shallow.

**Claude is better for building.** If you're trying to create systems — content pipelines, business automation, anything that requires sustained context and reliable output — Claude is where the momentum is.

And here's the part nobody's talking about: **Anthropic just launched Claude Import Memory.** As of today, free users can transfer their chat history from ChatGPT and Gemini into Claude. Your context, your preferences, your conversation patterns — they come with you. The switching cost just dropped to near zero.

That's not a coincidence. That's a company reading the moment and removing the last barrier.

## The Uncomfortable Part

I want to be honest about something. Building on Claude isn't all smooth.

There are days when the API goes down. Last Sunday, Claude had a global outage. My entire content pipeline froze. When you build your business on someone else's infrastructure, you inherit their downtime. I know this from 20 years of networking — single points of failure will always bite you.

Claude also lacks image generation. If I need visuals, I'm still going to other tools. And the free tier is genuinely limited — the context window shrinks, you hit rate limits fast. The paid plan isn't optional if you're doing real work.

But here's the thing. Every tool has trade-offs. The question isn't "which AI is perfect?" It's "which AI is improving in the direction I care about?"

For me, that direction is: reliable, honest, and good at building things. Claude is moving that way faster than anyone else right now.

## One Thing to Take Away

The AI tool you pick today isn't just a product choice. It's a bet on a company's values.

OpenAI is betting that government contracts and enterprise scale are the path forward. Anthropic is betting that trust and safety are competitive advantages, not constraints.

The App Store rankings suggest users are starting to have an opinion on which bet they prefer.

I made my choice six months ago. If you're still on the fence, today's a good day to try Claude. Import your ChatGPT history. Spend a week building something. See if the output is different.

I think you'll stay.
